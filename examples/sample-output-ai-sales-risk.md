# Sample Output: AI Sales Risk Tool GTM Decision Brief

## 1. Product Snapshot

**Product:** AI call summary and churn-risk follow-up tool for SaaS teams  
**Core use case:** Convert sales/onboarding call signals into follow-up tasks and churn-risk warnings  
**Current stage:** Prototype, no revenue  
**Desired outcome:** Choose a beachhead and run a 14-day validation test  
**Constraints:** Two-person team, no paid acquisition, founder-led outreach available  

## 2. Input Completeness Audit

**Completeness:** Medium-low  
**Strongest evidence:** 3 founder interviews and 20 waitlist signups  
**Missing critical evidence:** Willingness to pay, repeated buyer trigger, proof that churn-risk detection is more urgent than call summarization  
**Confidence impact:** Recommendation should be treated as a validation direction, not a scaling strategy  

## 3. Recommended Beachhead Segment

**Recommendation:** Seed-stage B2B SaaS founders doing founder-led sales and early customer success  
**Confidence:** Medium-low  
**Why this segment:** They are reachable through founder networks and outbound, feel follow-up pain personally, and can make fast purchase decisions. The team can validate this segment faster than CS managers or RevOps teams.

## 4. Rejected or Deferred Segments

| Segment | Decision | Reason | Revisit trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| CS managers at 50-200 employee SaaS | Defer | Likely stronger budget, but harder to reach and may require integrations/security proof | Revisit after 3 founder-led pilots produce workflow proof |
| Mid-market RevOps | Reject for now | Procurement, integrations, and incumbent tools create slow proof cycle | Revisit after product has CRM integrations and reference customers |

## 5. Recommended GTM Motion

**Primary motion:** Founder-led sales  
**Supporting motion:** Content-assisted outbound  
**Why this fits:** The team needs buyer learning more than scale. The buyer is identifiable, calls are feasible, and a prototype can be demonstrated manually.  
**Why other motions are weaker now:** PLG is premature because value depends on call data and workflow trust. Content-led is too slow for the 14-day goal. Mid-market outbound is too heavy for the current proof level.  
**Contraindication to watch:** If founders say the problem is annoying but not urgent, the motion should shift to a narrower trigger such as "post-onboarding handoff risk."  
**First action:** Build a 40-founder target list and offer a free call follow-up/churn-risk audit.  

## 6. Positioning Hypothesis

**For:** Seed-stage B2B SaaS founders  
**In this trigger situation:** They are running sales/onboarding calls themselves and losing follow-up context  
**Current alternative:** Manual notes, CRM tasks, call recorders, founder memory  
**Main pain:** Missed follow-ups and late discovery of churn risk  
**Why now:** Founder-led sales creates too many customer conversations to track manually  
**Why this product:** It turns call signals into follow-up actions without needing a full RevOps stack  
**Proof needed:** Founders must agree that missed follow-up is urgent enough to pay for before a mature CS team exists  

**One-line hypothesis:** For seed-stage SaaS founders doing founder-led sales, GTM Decision Lab would position this product as "the fastest way to turn customer calls into follow-up actions before churn risk gets buried."  

**Claims to avoid until proven:**

- "Predicts churn" unless there is retention validation
- "Works for RevOps teams" until mid-market workflows are tested
- "Replaces CS tools" without integration evidence

## 8. 14-Day Validation Plan

| Experiment | Hypothesis | Segment | Action | Success signal | Failure threshold | Evidence to collect | Decision rule |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Founder follow-up audit | Seed founders have urgent follow-up pain after customer calls | Seed-stage B2B SaaS founders | Offer 10 free audits using recent call notes or recordings | 5+ qualified calls, 3+ founders ask for repeated use, 1+ paid pilot discussion | Fewer than 2 qualified calls or founders say problem is low priority | Pain words, current workaround, willingness to pay, requested proof | Continue if urgency and willingness to pay appear; narrow trigger if interest is vague |

## 9. Decision Gates

**Continue if:** At least 3 target founders describe the same missed-follow-up pain and ask for a next step  
**Change segment if:** Founders like the idea but say a CS manager would own it later  
**Change motion if:** Outbound produces curiosity but no qualified meetings  
**Change positioning if:** Buyers care more about "follow-up automation" than "churn risk"
